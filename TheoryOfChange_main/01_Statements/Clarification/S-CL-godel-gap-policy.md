# Gödel-gap policy = hedge, probe, or jump plus operator training

AI_13 condenses the operational advice: whenever the current description hits a Gödel gap (can’t decide), choose among three actions—hedge (robust guard), probe (cheap information), or jump (invent a new variable). Teach operators (simulate, factorize, approximate, analogize) instead of narrow tricks so teams can recompose solutions when a gap appears. Gap literacy becomes a skill: mark bends vs. holes on the manifold, log hedge/probe/jump choices, and evaluate transfer by how well a small operator basis solves unseen tasks. Foundations, evolution, markets, law—all reuse the same policy: gaps are inevitable; progress comes from disciplined probes and timely Gödel jumps rather than pretending the hole isn’t there.
