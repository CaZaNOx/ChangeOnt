# Steps required to satisfy hardcore critics of the hard problem

To convince the toughest modal rationalists (lines 17660‑17740) you must deliver: (T1) explicit definability of phenomenal predicates from the base structure (Beth-style); (T2) rigidity/uniqueness so physical/structural isomorphism forces phenomenal isomorphism; (T3) a modal collapse showing \(P \land I \land \neg Q\) contradicts the CO axioms; plus a categorical equivalence \(F : C \to \Phi\) establishing that phenomenology and structure are the same object in two languages. Empirically, preregister invariants, prove independence from first-person vocabulary, produce novel predictions, and show your model compresses data better than rivals. Only then do zombies become incoherent and the “just a redefinition” charge loses force.
