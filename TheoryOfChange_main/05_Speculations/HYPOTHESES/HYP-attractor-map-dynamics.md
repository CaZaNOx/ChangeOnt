# Hypothesis — Attractor maps across ants/rules/substrates

Claim (speculative)
- Different dynamical rules (“ants”) may share attractor maps across substrates (grid/topology/coloring). Mapping which attractors are “strong” for which rule/topology could expose common structure and sensitivities.

Exploratory sketch
- Place multiple rule variants on fixed substrates; classify attractor destinations; map “attractor basins” as coordinates in a CO motif space. Vary substrate (2D/3D, coloring, obstacles) and see how attractor maps deform or vanish.

Questions/falsifiers
- Do shared attractors exist across rule families, or does each rule topologize the space differently? Can attractor maps predict basin membership without simulation?
