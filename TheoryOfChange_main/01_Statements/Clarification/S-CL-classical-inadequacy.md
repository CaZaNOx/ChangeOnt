# Classical fixed-state tools cannot model emergent structure

Markov chains and related classical models presuppose a fixed state space and cannot genuinely create new variables, dimensions, or frames mid-run. In your change ontology, new structure emerges from structural tension, collapse, and pointer failure, so the space itself can grow or reconfigure. Classical tools may only emulate this by smuggling in every possible decomposition ahead of time, which defeats the structural novelty we seek. This is the critical gap highlighted in AI_13 lines 900‑1030.

## Philosophical Justification
- CO allows variable birth/Tx; fixed-state models cannot express these without pre-enumerating all possibilities, which erases novelty.
- Emergence from GH/structural tension is incompatible with a static sigma-algebra/state set.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Classical approximations are acceptable only when the relevant births/Tx are provably absent or predeclared.
- Use this as a warning label: claims of “capturing emergence” must specify how new variables/frames enter the model.

## Next Steps in Chain
- suggest: pair with [[S-DF-prm-variable-birth]] audits and Tx algebra tests before using classical surrogates.
- suggest: document when/where classical emulation is permitted (bounded novelty regimes).
