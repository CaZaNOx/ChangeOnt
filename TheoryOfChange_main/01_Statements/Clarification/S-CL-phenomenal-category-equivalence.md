# Phenomenal states are category-equivalent to thick-present dynamics

Model the thick-present dynamical states as a category \(D\) whose morphisms are update flows that preserve the tuple \((G^*, U, B, A, \tau)\). Model phenomenal states as a category \(P\) whose morphisms capture introspective transitions and reportable similarities. AI_13 lines 16669‑16690 outline a functor \(F : D \to P\) that sends each dynamical state to its phenomenal class via the bridge law. Showing that \(F\) is full, faithful, and essentially surjective establishes \(P \simeq D / \!\!\sim\): the “mental” and “physical” descriptions are the same structure in two languages, leaving no remainder once the tuple is fixed.
