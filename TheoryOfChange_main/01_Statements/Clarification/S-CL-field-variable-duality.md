# Variables are evaluations of the change field, not the field itself

Change ontology distinguishes the subject-wide change field \(C : M \times \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}^k\) from the variables we track. A variable is the evaluation of \(C\) along a slice or embedding—attention, urgency, the self-gradient—but the field carries additional structure: locality, topology, smoothness, and gauge transformations. Conflating them loses the geometric content that makes PDEs, symmetries, and coarse-graining meaningful. Variables express field features (order parameters, pullbacks), while the field encodes the full thick-present evolution. The duality triple in lines 16778‑16782 formalizes this “two sides of one coin” view: a change field on the experience manifold, its variable-level projections, and the induced evolution/adjoint flows.
