# Gödel-hole density peaks at interfaces, predicting innovation zones

## Claim (formal)
Gödel-hole density is highest at interfaces/phase boundaries where local models fail; these regions predict innovation spikes and should concentrate Gödel-pressure diagnostics.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
Boundaries between regimes are gap‑rich; that’s where new structure tends to appear.

## Philosophical Justification
Well‑formed attractors admit adequate local models; interfaces do not, producing more GH. Since GH mark required structural extensions, innovation is likeliest where GH density peaks. Focusing diagnostics there aligns exploration with structural need.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Use GH maps and pressure scores to target boundary regions.
- Treat “smooth basins” as lower-priority for novelty unless disturbed by Tx.
