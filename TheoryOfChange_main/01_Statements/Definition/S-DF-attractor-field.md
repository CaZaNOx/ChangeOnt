# Attractor field / spiral dynamics
## Claim (formal)
Breath dynamics on identity spaces induce spiral approaches to attractors; SE shapes basin structure; HAQ yields families of discrete attractors.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
Patterns don’t just land; they orbit and settle in characteristic ways as the system breathes.

## Philosophical Justification
- [[S-DF-haq-core-family]]: discrete attractor families exist; breath plus SE determine which modes are occupied.
- [[S-DF-stabilization-energy]]: depth/variance of basins; low SE collapses shallow basins.
- [[S-DF-rtv-operator]]: recursive validation manifests as spiral approaches rather than one-shot convergence.

## Explanation (informal)
Imagine identity patterns circling closer with each breath. SE sets how “sticky” the basin is; HAQ says which basins exist; breath supplies the spiral dynamic.

## Derivation (Formal/Logical/Mathematical)
```text
x_{t+1} = F_breath(x_t) with SE-weighted damping
Spiral if eigenvalues are complex with |λ|<1; attractor families quantized via HAQ constraints.
```

- corollary: [[S-CR-stability-duality-via-attractors]], [[S-CR-spiral-context-continuity]]

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Spiral vs straight convergence depends on gauge/SE coupling; declare conditions when applying.
- Attractor fields can fracture under high tension/low SE, creating transient basins.
