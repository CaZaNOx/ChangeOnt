# Remaining gaps in the identity bridge must be closed explicitly

AI_13 lines 16600‑16628 list the unsolved pieces of the hard problem even after adopting the thick-present tuple: (1) the identity bridge “experience = self-gradient” remains a postulate that needs justification, not a derivation; (2) the modal zombie gap survives unless a no-inversion theorem makes differing qualia incoherent; (3) first-person givenness is still stipulated rather than compelled from structure; (4) grain and scope questions—what makes one subject instead of many overlapping loops—require precise bounds; and (5) uniqueness and multiple realizability need proofs that non-isomorphic mechanisms cannot realize the same profile without the same phenomenology. These constraints define the remaining work for the bridge law.
