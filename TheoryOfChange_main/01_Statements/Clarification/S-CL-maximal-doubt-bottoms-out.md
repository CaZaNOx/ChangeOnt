# Maximal doubt bottoms out at experiencing subjectivity

## Claim (formal)
Even under maximal doubt, the fact of experiencing (a minimal experiencing subject) remains undeniable; doubting itself presupposes an experiencing subject. “I experience” is epistemic bedrock.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
You can doubt anything else, but you can’t doubt that you are having experience as you doubt—doubt itself is an experience.

## Philosophical Justification
From [[01_Statements/FoundationalTruth/S-FT-immediate-datum]] and the triad [[01_Statements/Definition/S-DF-structure-of-experience]], doubting is an act within experience. Therefore, even maximal skepticism cannot undercut the presence of experience/subject; denying that would performatively reinstate it.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Sets the lower bound for skeptical moves; beyond this, performative contradiction occurs.
- This does not assert anything about the nature of contents, only that experience occurs.
