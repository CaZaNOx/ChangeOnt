# CO-on-CO meta-operators — applying A–H to any element

## Claim (formal)
Each core letter/operator (A–H) can act meta-recursively on any CO element X (statement, motif, variable, gauge, tolerance), producing a closed “CO-on-CO” protocol: gauge X (A→X), open holes for X (B→X), preserve identity of X (C→X), manage precision/density of X (H→X), etc. This recursion is well-formed because the operators are defined on change structures and compose under the same bend/quotient algebra.

## Sketch of the eight meta-operators
- A→X (Gauge on X): reweight X’s parts by predictiveness/usefulness.
- B→X (Hole/variable on X): admit new degrees of freedom where X fails.
- C→X (Identity on X): track re-identification of X across bends.
- D→X (Density/entropy on X): measure X’s change rate/dispersion.
- E→X (Multi-scale patterns on X): expose nested structure within X.
- F→X (Directional cost on X): encode anisotropic bend costs for X.
- G→X (Probabilistic layer on X): wrap X in probabilistic spreads if churn dominates.
- H→X (Classical collapse on X): force X to its crisp limit when conditions trigger.
