# Generalization principles across domains
## Claim (formal)
Across domains, CO applies by (i) expressing identity via self‑similarity/invariants, (ii) recognizing Tx where frames shift, (iii) validating via RTV/SRL, (iv) tracking GH for honest non‑closure.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
We don’t carry a fixed template; we carry procedures for recognizing persistence, meta‑change, validation, and limits.

## Philosophical Justification
- Identity is domain‑specific (cf. [[S-DF-identity-through-change]]): persistence rules must be explicit to transport claims.
- Frame shifts (Tx) vary by domain; making them explicit prevents smuggling fixed frames.
- Validation (RTV/SRL) binds claims to domain feedback rather than analogy alone.
- GH pressure reminds us to disclose limits/closure gaps; cross‑domain use must track where reasoning breaks.
These principles are necessary to avoid unjustified universality claims and maintain auditability when moving between physics/ML/bio/social domains.

## Derivation (Philosophical)
- Start with identity-through-change to declare invariants/Sim per domain.
- Add domain-specific Tx operators and compatibility conditions.
- Attach RTV/SRL probes to observable benchmarks for that domain.
- Record GH markers where closure assumptions fail; restrict transfer accordingly.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- “Applies across domains” means a domain card exists listing invariants, Tx classes, validation benchmarks, SE budgets, breath scales, and GH notes.
- Without those declarations, claims are heuristic and should not be labeled stable.
- Domain cards should include symbol instantiations if notation differs (e.g., different Δ or Sim metrics).
