# Distinguish the generator, occurrence, and phenomenological faces of change

## Statement (clarification)
“Change” plays three roles: generator \(G\) (cause/operator), occurrence \(Δ\) (event), and presented contrast \(π\) (phenomenological difference). Interfaces must be explicit: presentation \(P(C;S)\) for subject‑relative contrasts, generator face \(C[G]\) for ontic dynamics, meta face \(C[M]\) for change‑of‑change (curvature/renormalization). Bridges (e.g., presented contrast ⇒ generator exists) must be declared to avoid equivocation.

## Philosophical Justification
- From immediate datum, we meet contrasts \(π\); asserting generators or meta-change requires bridging assumptions.
- Mixing faces without stating interfaces yields tautological “everything changes” with no audit trail.
- Explicit faces keep metrics, proofs, and phenomenology aligned and falsifiable.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Principle of Sufficient Differentiation: observed \(π\) implies some generator; must be stated, not assumed.
- Meta face (curvature/renormalization) should cite Tx/ΔΔ operators where used.
- Presentation face is subject-relative; tie to awareness/experience nodes when deployed.
