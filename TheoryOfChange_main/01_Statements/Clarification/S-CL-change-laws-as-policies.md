# Physical laws are change policies (gate, align, carry, curve, grain)

## Statement (clarification)
Physical “laws” are compact policies that bias change: Gate (causal cones / signal speed), Align (preserve invariants/gauges), Carry (conservation/inertia), Curve (drift/curvature), Grain (set resolution/options). They are not object catalogs but constraints/weights on paths.

## Philosophical Justification
- From CO‑0, what persists are policies shaping change; treating laws as policy blocks avoids reifying substances.
- Gate/Align/Carry/Curve/Grain cover causal speed, gauge coherence, inertial tendencies, curvature, and resolution—capturing familiar physics effects in change-first terms.
- Selection favors policies with horizons and few knobs, explaining universality/criticality.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- EM as phase-consistency (Align + Gate); gravity shares Lipschitz/causal bound; analogies (poker/evolution) illustrate the same operators.
- Effective laws can drift; policy view anticipates slow parameter changes.
- Ties to gauge audits and causal-cone tests.
