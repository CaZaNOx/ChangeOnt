# Distinguish true Gödel results from expressivity gaps and define the term

AI_13 cautions against “Gödel everywhere.” Gödel’s theorems concern arithmetic theories; most real cases are limited expressivity or computability constraints. Define a Gödel hole precisely: given a system \(S\), a language/model \(L\), and a solver \(T\) in \(L\), it is a task-relevant subset where \(T\) cannot decide/compute the property even though the property has determinate truth in \(S\). Then log the three canonical sources—Gödel/Tarski undecidability, expressivity gaps, or computability limits. Anything else (innovation pressure, creative leaps) must be justified by reduction to these mechanisms or renamed. This keeps Gödel references honest and tells you when to reach for computability theory versus categorical or dynamical tools.
