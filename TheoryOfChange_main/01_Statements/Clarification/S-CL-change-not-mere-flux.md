# Change is structured transformation, not mere flux
## Claim (formal)
Δ denotes structured transformation relative to invariants and constraints, not untyped “mere difference.”

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
Change is always “something changing under some structure,” not chaos. Invariants, tolerances, and reachability criteria keep Δ intelligible.

## Philosophical Justification
Without declared invariants and tolerances (see [[01_Statements/Definition/S-DF-identity-invariants]]), Δ would collapse into noise and could not ground identity or prediction. Given [[01_Statements/Derivation/S-DR-change-cannot-begin]] and [[01_Statements/FoundationalTruth/S-FT-continuity-noncessation]], intelligible description requires foregrounding constraints so that continuity yields persistent, referable patterns.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- When invoking Δ, specify the invariants (≈ thresholds, conserved relations) that make a transformation recognizable.
- “Flux” is permitted as variation, not as denial of structure.
- Ties into identity formation via [[01_Statements/Definition/S-DF-identity-invariants]].
