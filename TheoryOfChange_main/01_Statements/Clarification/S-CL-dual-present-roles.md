# Thick-present experience splits into sensory and reflexive roles

Within the thick present window \(\tau\) the subject carries at least two concurrent roles: \(I_{\text{sense}}(t)\), the broadcasted now-state, and \(I_{\text{ref}}(t)\), the comparator that maps past and anticipated futures back into the present. Their difference \(\Delta I_t = I_{\text{ref}}(t) - I_{\text{sense}}(t)\) is tagged by urgency \(U_t\) and scales the self-deformation gain \(\lambda_t\). Phenomenologically this can feel like “two I’s” co-present, yet it is a single subject whose roles collapse when \(|\Delta I_t|\) shrinks (e.g., in deep meditation). Recognizing this split explains how felt duality, salience, and adaptation arise from one loop, as described in AI_13 lines 16576‑16584.
