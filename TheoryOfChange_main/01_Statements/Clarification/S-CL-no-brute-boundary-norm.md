# No-Brute-Boundary norm makes “no first change” an explicit CO axiom

AI_13 clarifies that Change Ontology must own its “no first change” stance by spelling out CO-1 / No-First (NF) and the No-Brute-Boundary (NBB) norm. Without those axioms, boundary-first-change models remain logically consistent: rivals can treat a first tick as a primitive boundary condition or a brute miracle. Declaring NF+NBB says, methodologically, that every apparent boundary must be modeled as seeded change (phase transitions, horizons, latent drift), and that brute events require repeatable evidence before admission. The note also partitions optional modules—Scale-Refinement (no minimal Δ) and density assumptions—so continuity claims are flagged as extra commitments rather than hidden deductions.
