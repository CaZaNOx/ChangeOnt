# CO relaxes classical differential assumptions via nonsmooth flows

AI_13 addresses the concern about importing classical smooth dynamical assumptions: while earlier formulas used Picard–Lindelöf settings, CO can be formalized with accretive operators and Crandall–Liggett semigroup generators (monotone inclusions on state manifolds), hybrid inclusions (pieces defined by viability kernels), and differential inclusions that allow creation/merging. Expectations operators are then stochastic integrals, and gradients are via subdifferentials. Gödel regions become set-valued gap sets. Thus the change operator \( \mathcal{C} \) maps contexts to sets/distributions of nexts, covering smooth patches and nonsmooth jumps without smuggling in classical substance assumptions.

## Philosophical Justification
- Avoids sneaking in smoothness: nonsmooth/semi-group/differential inclusion formalisms cover creation/merging and gaps.
- Keeps CO compatible with discontinuities while retaining analytic handles where possible.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Formal tools: accretive operators, Crandall–Liggett semigroups, hybrid inclusions, subdifferentials, set-valued gaps.
- Connects to continuity logic: choose assumptions explicitly; use nonsmooth frameworks when smoothness is unjustified.
