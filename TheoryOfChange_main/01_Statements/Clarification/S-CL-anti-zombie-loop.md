# Anti-zombie constraint ties phenomenology to the thick-present tuple

## Statement (clarification)
Zombie objections lose force when experience is tied to a thick-present tuple \((G^*, U, B, A, \tau)\): self-gradient, urgency/valence, broadcast clarity, reflexive access, temporal span. Two systems matching this tuple cannot diverge in felt experience without diverging in adaptation, because the same tagged gradient drives plasticity and reports. Residual “ineffable” is modeled as Gödel pressure \(G_t\), not an extra fact.

## Philosophical Justification
- From immediate datum and unified structure-of-experience, phenomenology co-varies with the functional tuple; holding it fixed fixes the experiential profile.
- Gödel pressure accounts for mismatch without invoking non-physical qualia.
- A “phenomenon-less twin” either breaks tuple parity or yields identical traces, undermining the zombie move.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- The tuple must be operationalized: how to measure \(G^*, U, B, A, \tau\); otherwise parity claims are empty.
- Applies within the thick present; long-term divergence can occur if tuple components drift.
- Ties phenomenology to auditables; zombie talk becomes a claim about measurement errors, not metaphysics.

## Next Steps in Chain
- suggest: define estimators for each tuple component and log parity tests.
- suggest: connect \(G_t\) (GH pressure) to variable-birth logs in mismatch regions.
