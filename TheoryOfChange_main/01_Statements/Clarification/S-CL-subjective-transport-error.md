# Use Subjective Transport Error to evaluate intersubject alignment

After learning canonical latent roles \(Z^*\) and subject warps \(W_i\), AI_13 proposes a measurable score—Subjective Transport Error (STE)—to quantify how well interventions commute across people. STE aggregates the mismatch between predicted and observed phenomenal shifts when the same objective knob is applied (after unwarping); the gauge residue records variation left after the best \(\phi \in G\). Low STE means the “satellite” latent reproduces each subject’s experience geometry with bounded distortion; high STE signals language leakage, nonstationarity, or missing variables. Tracking STE over time (with drift terms \(W_{i,t}\)), warping states via \(z_j = W_j \circ W_i^{-1}(z)\), preregistering commuting counterfactuals, and shrinking \(G\) when it swallows distinctions keep the alignment program honest.
