# Process-first CO axioms exclude the 4-D block interpretation

AI_13 makes the distinction explicit: A0–A3 (“perpetual becoming,” endogenous generator, openness, no minimal delta) define a process ontology in which state \(x(t)\), generator \(G\), and slowly drifting law manifold \(\lambda\) never halt. Under those axioms the tenseless 4‑D block view is incompatible: a block assumes a completed history with no motor, whereas CO requires nonvanishing generator energy and law plasticity at every cut. The text clarifies the logic: the block picture is logically consistent by itself, but once you adopt the CO axiom stack it is ruled out; conversely, the axioms themselves remain substantive commitments, not logical necessities. This keeps the chain of entailment clean—“change now ⇒ block contradiction” becomes “change now + A0–A3 ⇒ block contradiction.”
