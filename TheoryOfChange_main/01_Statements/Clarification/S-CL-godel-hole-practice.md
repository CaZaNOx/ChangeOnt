# Gödel holes are practical compression gaps that predict where new layers help

AI_13 reframes Gödel holes as locales where current formalisms fail to stably compress or predict, and where adding a new variable/level reduces residuals. The idea unifies incompleteness, underdetermination, intractability, and representation mismatch: each is a flavor of “hole pressure.” Rather than claiming “holes in nature,” point to domains where the machinery plugs in (layer birth, innovation triggers) and emphasize the measured expressivity upgrade. This grounds the metaphor and highlights where the Gödel toolkit should be deployed.
