# Minimal CO framework for consciousness joins change axioms and predictions

The summary (lines 19713‑19792) sets out a concrete blueprint: Change Ontology posits a single change-first ontological primitive, the ES tuple (Subject, Structure-of-Experience, Content), structural contradiction (SC) yielding structural facts, and a hard-problem stance that replaces “why feel?” with a gauge-mapping problem between first- and third-person invariants. Gödel pressure is operationalized as persistent structured residuals that predict layer jumps. The framework lists empirical predictions (stakes modulate learning and felt intensity; counterfactual channels matter; remaps change felt space; flow signatures appear in precision/attention patterns), falsifiers, and a minimal research program (perturb-and-return, counterfactual lesions, manifold remaps, statistical attractors) plus gauge-map alignment goals. This ties the ontology to testable science.

- Gödel pressure as residuals links novelty predictions to structured mismatches.
