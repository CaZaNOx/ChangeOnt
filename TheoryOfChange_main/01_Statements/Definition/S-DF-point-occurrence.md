# Point / Occurrence — generalized happening independent of subject
## Claim (formal)
Point(x) denotes a generalized occurrence implied by change, without requiring a subject. Experienced happening is the subject-conditioned instance of Point.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
Things can happen as part of change whether or not a subject is there to experience them.

## Philosophical Justification
- From [[S-FT-immediate-datum]] and [[S-DF-arisal]], change brings things into presence; that structural capacity does not depend on a witnessing subject.
- To later define subjectivity, we need a notion of “happening” that can be constrained by but not reduced to subject experience.

## Explanation (informal)
Point/occurrence is the neutral placeholder for “a happening.” It allows us to talk about structure (reach, locality, eventlets) without presuming personal awareness.

## Derivation (Philosophical)
- Given Δ and arisal, assert existence of entities/events x that become present; strip any subject index to obtain the generalized Point(x).

## Derivation (Formal/Logical/Mathematical)
```text
Δ ⇒ ∃x : Present(x)          // existence of occurrences
Point(x) := Present(x) without subject-qualification
```

## Proofs/Corollaries References
- corollary: supports defining pointers and reach over occurrences.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Subjective happenings = Point ∩ experienced; we keep them distinct to avoid anthropocentric bias in later formalism.
- Provides the ground vocabulary for eventlets (minimal discernible occurrences).
