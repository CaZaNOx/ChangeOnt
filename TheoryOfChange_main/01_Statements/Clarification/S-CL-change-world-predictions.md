# Change-ontology worldview yields testable commitments and falsifiers

## Statement (clarification)
Under CO‑0 + No‑Brute‑Boundary, the world should show: (i) every change seeded by prior change; (ii) continuity at substrate with discreteness as coarse cuts; (iii) attractors as identities; (iv) higher layers as coordinate compressions; (v) Gödel pressure where new variables/layers are born. Test bets: affect dampening slows error‑weighted learning; hole‑density metrics (compression/MDL deficits, Fisher‑curvature) predict emergence; attractor strength forecasts persistence. Falsifiers: brute events with no precursors, a perfectly predictable fine‑grain atlas (no holes), or failure of the hole/attractor forecasts.

## Philosophical Justification
- From ongoing change and identity‑through‑change, persistence must ride on attractors, not essences.
- No‑Brute‑Boundary rejects uncaused discrete spikes; discreteness must be a cut of continuous flow.
- Gödel pressure operationalizes modeling limits; expecting it predicts where structure will grow.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- These are conditional, not established facts; they form an audit list for CO.
- Hole density can be probed via MDL deficits or curvature; affect/attention coupling is measurable.
- Coordinate compressions = domain-specific coarse maps; declare the compression when testing.
