# Prove existence, uniqueness, and representation of the phenomenal normal form

The entailment program requires: (1) existence and uniqueness of \(NF\) given the gauge group \(G\) and normalization constraints; (2) representation theorems showing each admissible subject \(x\) has exactly one map \(\Phi_x\) satisfying the constraints; (3) failure cases (non-unique \(NF\), underspecified gauge) invalidate the closure. These proof obligations turn the normal-form definition into a rigorous bridge.
