# Map Gödel-hole proxies to predict re-patterning and regime shifts

AI_13 details how to operationalize Gödel holes in practice: treat rising residual ratios \(R\) versus invariant drift as a hole-density proxy; log under-determination (different \(\theta\) explaining the same past but diverging futures) as “lens-space” Gödel holes; detect when \(R_{\text{lens}}\) climbs without lens drift—signaling a genuine structural change; and use proxy maps to anticipate where new invariants emerge, where breakdowns will occur, and when to deploy many-valued/modal logic (“currently undecidable”) locally. This makes Gödel holes actionable in experiments and monitoring dashboards.
