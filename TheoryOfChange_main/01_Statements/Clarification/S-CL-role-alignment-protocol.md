# Align subjective roles via satellite-to-subject manifold transforms

The RPG “role alignment” plan (AI_13 lines 17019‑17079) describes how to learn transforms between objective role parameters \(R\) (“satellite” knobs) and each subject’s experiential manifold \(E_i\): estimate baseline manifolds \(S_i\) and latent role bases \(U_i\), collect multimodal evidence (reports, behavior, physiology), fit subject-specific transforms \(\Phi_i\) constrained to preserve topology/compositionality, and factor a canonical role map \(A^*\) plus per-subject deformations \(W_i\) (Procrustes/CCA/cycle-consistent models) so \(\Phi_i \approx W_i \circ \Phi^*\). Use the learned maps to request target phenomenology, compensate for natural biases, and check commuting interventions—tweaking the same role knob should move \(E_i\) in predicted directions across contexts. Drift terms \(\Phi_{i,t}\) capture learning; failure to align exposes remaining gauge freedom or missing variables.
