# Gödel-hole framing describes the boundary conditions selecting conscious loops
## Claim (formal)
Gödel holes act as boundary conditions that select for conscious loops: finite agents encountering unmodellable pockets must perform meta‑control (gating, binding, reweighting, representation spawning) concentrated at those gaps.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
The “edge of what we can model” is where consciousness works hardest; GH make that edge visible.

## Philosophical Justification
Given persistent GH (unclosable gaps), survival and coherence require loops that manage capacity, adjust representations, and redirect attention. [[01_Statements/Clarification/S-CL-predictive-loop-control-profile]] sketches that loop; GH framing explains why such loops are favored—because open worlds force recurrent meta‑work at boundaries.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- GH are a lens on constraints, not an alternative ontology.
- Expect intensified phenomenology near GH because meta‑control load spikes there.
- Managing GH requires explicit capacity limits and structured spawning of new representations.
