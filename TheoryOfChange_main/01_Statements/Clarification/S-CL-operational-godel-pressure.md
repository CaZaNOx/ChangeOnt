# Operational Gödel pressure links residuals, invariants, and experiments

AI_13 formalizes layer forcing: Gödel pressure \(\Gamma\) at layer \(k\) is defined via persistent residuals in a model class (normed difference between dynamics and self-model weighted by complexity), and its subject-level effect is urgency \(w_t\) driving structure learning in specific SoE subspaces. Tube attractor criteria involve transverse contraction \(\lambda_\perp < 0\) and drift bounds. Experimental protocols (P1–P3) include counterfactual lesions, remapping qualia with known Jacobians, and manifold gauge matching; falsifiers are dissociations between \(w_t\) and learning/phenomenology. Gauge maps \(G: \mathcal{M}_{1P} \leftrightarrow \mathcal{O}_{3P}\) must preserve invariants (geodesic lengths, curvature). A minimal research program follows: perturb-and-return, counterfactual lesions, remaps, statistical attractors, and shared gauge invariants.
