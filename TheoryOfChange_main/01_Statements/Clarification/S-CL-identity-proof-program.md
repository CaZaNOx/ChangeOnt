# Roadmap for uniqueness, identity thesis, and no-zombie proofs

Lines 17451‑17487 lay out the theoretical lifts still required: (1) prove uniqueness/minimality—show the typed-error/gauge model is the lowest-complexity system matching all cross-agent data, otherwise a simpler non-conscious model might suffice; (2) adopt an explicit identity thesis (“phenomenal character = gauge-fixed control/appraisal class”) and defend it; (3) craft a no-zombie-within-class theorem so any system duplicating the dynamics must share the invariants; (4) demonstrate systematic interventions that move experience as predicted across individuals with quantified error bars. These targets turn operational closure into a defensible theory.
