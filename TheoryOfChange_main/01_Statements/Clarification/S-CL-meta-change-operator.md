# Meta-change tracks operator drift, distinction growth, and Gödel-pressure tests

AI_13 highlights that meta-change means the update operator \(\mathcal{C}_t\) itself drifts (\(\mathcal{C}_{t+\Delta} \neq \mathcal{C}_t\)), observable via bursts in distinction growth and operator drift preceding new regimes. Hypotheses: systems with higher operator drift recover faster from shocks and generate more novelty; subjective intensity should track gradients of model update. Gödel-hole pressure is operationally “no viable continuation”—predict hole-density spikes before vocabulary or feature growth. Suggested experiments involve tracking operator changes, measuring hole-pressure, and comparing against fixed-operator baselines. Falsifiers: no correlation between hole density and innovation, or equal resilience with fixed operators.
