# Hypothesis — Numbers and operators as process lenses

Claim (speculative)
- Treat reals and basic operators (+, −, ×, ÷, exp, sqrt) as change primitives with attached thresholds/error/compute cost, rather than static scalars. Negatives, zeros, infinities correspond to process directions, neutral elements, or divergence regimes in CO terms.

Exploratory sketch
- Define CO-native interpretations of operators (e.g., + as bend composition, × as repeated composition, − as inverse bend, exp as flow under a generator). Model a “number” as a process window with tolerance and cost (precision depth). Explore whether this reframing yields useful diagnostics beyond standard algebra.

Questions/falsifiers
- Does this reduce to standard algebra with no added insight? Can it predict stability/instability under varying precision/cost? Does it inform when classical operations fail under drift?
