# Hypothesis — CO creative reframing for math constructs

Claim (speculative)
- Treating math objects (reals, primes, constants) as frozen views of underlying change processes may yield new intuition pumps, but only if tied to explicit mechanisms and falsifiers; otherwise it risks mere relabeling.

Exploratory guardrails
- Demand operational definitions (process-first) and invariance/falsifiers for any reframed construct.
- Use CO’s bend/gauge/closure language to explain why a construct appears (e.g., thresholds, resource limits, attractor regimes), not just to rename it.

Why it matters
- Sets a seriousness bar for creative CO extensions: keep them in the speculative bucket until mechanisms and tests are explicit.
