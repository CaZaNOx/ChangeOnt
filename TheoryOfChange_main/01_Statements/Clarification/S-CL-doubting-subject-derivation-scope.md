# Doubting-subject derivation yields specific bridges and limits
## Claim (formal)
The doubting-subject (DS) derivation yields: (i) subjects as attractor-like loops preserving invariants while reallocating attention/prediction; (ii) cross-subject compression tests for shared structure; (iii) world hypotheses as explanations of inter-subject invariants; (iv) empirical predictions (e.g., variance/autocorrelation spikes) before new layers stabilize. It does not by itself entail ontic continuity or no-first-change; those require additional axioms.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
From “I experience change now,” DS licenses certain bridges and tests, but it stops short of global metaphysical claims unless extra premises are added.

## Philosophical Justification
Starting from [[01_Statements/FoundationalTruth/S-FT-immediate-datum]] and using DS/RDS ([[01_Statements/Clarification/S-CL-doubting-subject-methodology]]), we can model subjects as stabilization loops, test structure via role transforms, and predict early-warning signatures. However, claims like “change never began” or “change never ends” are not derivable from DS alone; they need further axioms (e.g., continuity/non-cessation). This keeps the phenomenological derivation honest and bounded.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Bridges: attractor-like subject loops; cross-subject compression; invariants-as-hypotheses.
- Limits: no automatic license to ontic continuity/no-first-change; must cite the relevant axioms separately.
- Predictions: expect volatility spikes when new layers form; use as empirical checks.
