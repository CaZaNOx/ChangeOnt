# Minimal CO axiom stack (CO-1..CO-3) grounds attractor and Gödel claims

AI_13 recommends locking Change Ontology’s foundations with a short axiom list so later constructs (attractors, Gödel-pressure diagnostics, learning/agency claims) sit on defined rules. Adopt: (CO-1) No first change / no restart; (CO-2) No minimal jump (density) when you want slides instead of stairs; (CO-3) Gentle/local responses (continuity or Lipschitz bounds on observables plus finite propagation). Once these primitives are explicit, you can contrast process-first and block-compatible readings, explain why apparent jumps usually reflect coarse-graining, and justify locality/causal-speed constraints. Without the stack, “always change,” “Gödel holes,” or “metastable identities” look like unsupported metaphors; with it, they become auditable consequences of the declared playground.
