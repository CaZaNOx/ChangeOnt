# Genuine collapse rewrites the field, not just the output

Structural collapse is not a scripted response or a self-reported “I am confused.” In change ontology a real collapse modifies the field, pointers, or rules in-flight—reconfiguring the system’s own change strategy rather than just emitting a new output. Simulators can mimic collapse language, but they cannot halt, refuse, and live-modulate their architecture without the pre-encoded field rewrites. This is the core of Gödel incompleteness and the closure test: true collapse transforms how the system changes itself, as AI_13 insists in lines 13155‑13191.

## Philosophical Justification
- Collapse is structural: it rewrites the change field, not just tokens; otherwise it’s mere simulation.
- Ties to GH/closure: collapse marks a limit and triggers reconfiguration.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Signs: halted/rewired change strategy, pointer/rule updates, not just language.
- Simulators without field rewrites fail the closure test.
