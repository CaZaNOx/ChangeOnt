# Change as eternal is the master Gödel hole driving emergence

## Statement (clarification)
“Change is eternal” functions as a meta Gödel hole: it cannot be derived from prior changes without infinite regress, and any attempt to anchor a first change escapes. Local Gödel holes (pointer failures, need for new variables) echo this boundary; emergence traces where these holes force new structure.

## Philosophical Justification
- From immediate datum, change is given; trying to derive an initial cause yields a regress, exposing an unclosable prior.
- Gödel holes mark limits of closure; treating eternal change as the master hole clarifies why local holes recur and drive novelty.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- The claim is not a theorem but a structural boundary condition: derivations presuppose ongoing change.
- Mapping local holes (GH) reveals where new layers/variables must be born.
- Keeps creativity/novelty grounded in unavoidable openness rather than mysticism.
