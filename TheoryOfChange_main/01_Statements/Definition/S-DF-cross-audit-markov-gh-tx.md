# Cross-audit protocol — Markov closure, Tx, and Gödel holes
## Claim (formal)
Given a reasoning/modeling chain C:
1) Detect any Tx (frame-shift) steps; if none, Markov closure may be acceptable (subject to fit).
2) If Tx present and model assumes fixed S with T:S→S, flag nonclosure and mark GH at the step needing extension.
3) Require explicit representation of Tx or justified abstraction that preserves identity and reach under declared criteria.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
If the problem requires changing the “space itself,” fixed‑space models are pretending. Mark that point as a Gödel hole and either extend the structure or acknowledge limits.

## Pseudocode
```text
for step in Chain:
  if DetectTx(step):
    if AssumesFixedSpace(step):
      Mark(GH, step); Require(TxSpec or Justification)
```

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Detection can use pointer audits (⇘²), invariants, and LocalReach compositionality checks.
