# Subject-field roles map to a compact operator library with explicit instrumentation

AI_13 makes the “role = shape in subject-field” story operational. Emotions are not vibes—they are control channels that reweight path ensembles, stretch/shrink horizons, and modulate invention hazard. Instrument a live agent by logging \(v\) (value ± prediction error), urgency \(u\), confidence \(c\), and the dominant emotion per decision; if the predicted bends (horizon shifts, risk appetite, exploration gates) track those signals, you have evidence the emotion basis is the right abstraction. On the actuation side, the role library collapses to a handful of canonical operators on the attention/shape field \(A\) and style \( \phi\): morphology edits (union/intersection via level sets), frontier-turn, inside-decay, global bias, entropy bump, re-entry penalty, periodic nudges, plus derived operations (rotate, phase, scale, convolve, gated multiplication). These operators describe playstyles, predict compatibility, and transfer across RL/emergent systems precisely because they act on the same path-logic geometry. Treat them as parameterized corridors/gates rather than metaphors so simulations and real agents can be audited against the same operator catalogue.
