# Hypothesis — Periodicity/rhythm coverage and torus gaps

Claim (speculative)
- Multiple periodic processes (clocks/rhythms) induce coverage patterns on a “torus” of events; gaps in coverage correspond to new frequencies/“primes” emerging. Deformations (angles, coupling) between rhythms create shared events and free space for new dimensions.

Exploratory sketch
- Model two or more periodicities as loops on a torus; track uncovered regions and how adding a new period changes coverage density. Relate this to emergence of new primes/gaps and to drift sensitivity in composite rhythms.

Questions/falsifiers
- Does this picture produce any predictive power (e.g., about where gaps appear) beyond existing number-theoretic or signal-processing results? Or is it just metaphor?
