# Δ-field tension — modulation pressure
## Claim (formal)
Δ-field tension quantifies pressure driving modulation and collapse; interacts with SE and RTV to shape stability.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
How strongly the field “wants to change” affects whether identities hold and truths stabilize.

## Philosophical Justification
- [[S-DF-identity-through-change]]: identity is maintained against ongoing Δ; tension measures the counterpressure Δ exerts.
- [[S-DF-rtv-operator]]: validation/breath cycles absorb or amplify tension; high tension with low RTV leads to collapse, adequate RTV can channel it into transformation.
- Without an explicit tension term, SE and entropy models cannot predict when identities destabilize.

## Explanation (informal)
Tension is the “restlessness” of the change field. If it builds faster than a system can validate and stabilize, identities fracture; if it is modulated, the system can redirect change into coherent updates.

## Derivation (Philosophical)
- Continuous Δ ⇒ deviations accumulate; quantify deviation pressure as tension.
- Couple tension to stabilization energy (SE) and entropy production to model whether persistence or collapse follows.

## Derivation (Formal/Logical/Mathematical)
```text
Tension := dΔ/dt projected onto identity-relevant modes.
Collapse risk when Tension > SE_buffer(RTV window).
```

## Proofs/Corollaries References
- corollary: feeds [[S-DF-stabilization-energy]] thresholds and [[S-DF-entropy-co]] dynamics.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Tension can be local or field-level; aggregate models must state scale.
- High tension is not bad per se; it can drive innovation if SE and RTV are sufficient.
