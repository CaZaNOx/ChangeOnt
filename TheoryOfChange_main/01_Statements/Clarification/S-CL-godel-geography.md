# Gödel-thick reality has heavy-tailed hole geography and depth metrics

## Statement (clarification)
Reality is Gödel-thick: workable regions interlaced with heavy-tailed hole distributions (many small, few large). Closing a big hole spawns staircases of smaller ones. Hole depth = persistence under reparameterizations; track which holes survive framework changes. Classify holes (benign, creative, hazardous, mirage) with matching actions (hedge, probe, jump). Deep holes forecast where new layers/variables will appear; shallow holes suggest quick reparameterization.

## Philosophical Justification
- From CO‑0 and GH framing, closure is local; expecting a heavy-tailed hole geography avoids pretending completeness.
- Depth as reparameterization-resilience matches the ontology: if a gap survives coordinate changes, it signals structural novelty rather than artifact.
- Explicit taxonomies/actions keep GH discussion actionable and auditable.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Log hole pressure over time; staircases after major closures are expected.
- Use depth metrics in audit: which gaps persist across frames? which vanish with better coordinates?
- Tie subjective reports (“steering into gaps”) to operational probes.

## Next Steps in Chain
- suggest: integrate hole taxonomy into audit tooling and variable-birth triggers.
- suggest: report depth measures alongside model revisions (Layer 12 practice).
