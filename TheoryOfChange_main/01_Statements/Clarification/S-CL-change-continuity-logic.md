# Continuity follows only with explicit No-Leap and No-Restart axiom

AI_13’s “peace treaty” shows how to make the logical structure explicit: Without extra axioms, “change is happening now” only rules out global stasis, not discrete ticks. Continuity is forced only if you adopt (NS) No-Leap (every change decomposes into smaller pre-changes) and No-Restart (no global stop followed by restart). Equivalent formulations include dense orders, “no minimal jump,” or forbidding atomic events via bounded change-rate or finite-bandwidth assumptions. The text lists countermodels—piecewise-constant flows, jump processes, fast-slow thresholds, latent-drift restarts—where change resumes after stasis without contradiction, proving that “change now ⇒ no first change” is not a theorem. The advice is to separate tautologies (no global stasis) from commitments (refinability, dense time), articulate optional modules (continuity option C), and note that “no minimal Δ” alone gives scale-freeness, not continuity. Discrete models remain coherent by interpreting “change” as hazard updates, hybrid systems, or set-valued kernels. Accepting the extra smoothness axioms yields the Continuity from Change lemma; otherwise discreteness is empirically, not logically, ruled out.
