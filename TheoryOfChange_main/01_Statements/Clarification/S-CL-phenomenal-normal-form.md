# Define phenomenal character as the gauge-fixed normal form of self-indexed dynamics

AI_13 proposes axioms (C1–C5) where change is the only primitive and subjectivity is a self-indexed subdynamics carved out by invariants. A phenomenal functor maps each subject \(x\) to a canonical normal form \(NF(x)\) obtained by quotienting its self-indexing/attention/appraisal configuration by the internal gauge symmetries. “What-it-is-like” at state \(s\) is identified with this gauge-fixed normal form, not with photons or external labels. To get an a priori entailment, show that any two change-ontology models agreeing on CO-facts must agree on these normal forms; uniqueness up to the stipulated gauge group is essential.
