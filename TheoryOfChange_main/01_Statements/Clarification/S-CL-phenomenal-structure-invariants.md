# Map phenomenal structure to state-space invariants and benchmark it

Closing the hard problem empirically requires an isomorphism between phenomenal similarity/contrast structure and invariants of the broadcast/self-model state space. Lines 16840‑16906 lay out the program: extract invariants of \(b_t\) (topology, metric relations, curvature), align them across subjects and agents (Procrustes/CCA), ablate self-inclusion or valence knobs to verify predicted collapses in phenomenology, and pit the resulting model against GNW/IIT/HOTT on shared datasets. Counterfactuals about access, report, learning, and felt drift must covary with these invariants; if reports or physiology dissociate systematically, the bridge fails. This roadmap turns “qualia space” into structure-preserving transforms between dynamics and experience.
