# Run local-truth ledgers and gauge trials to discipline attention

AI_13 calls out two failure patterns and their fixes. First: attention keeps selecting the most underdetermined philosophical questions (continuity, first change, “closure of the hard problem”) and postpones falsifiable work. Remedy: keep a seven-day “local-truth ledger”—three tiny predictions per day (toy processes, social nudges, microsims), track hit rates, mark surprise/corrections, and insist on closing loops before chasing new Gödel bait. Second: reflexive rejection of classical tools for fear of smuggled assumptions. Treat tools as gauges, not gods: adopt one classical gauge (e.g., KL as bend cost) for a week, preregister when it is allowed, log what it predicts, and keep it only if it delivers useful signals. Both drills translate to CO-4 (“change any arrow to change the loop”): auditing expectations, meanings, body states, or action tendencies is how you alter the breath loop rather than fantasize. Ledger plus gauge trials keep the program test-heavy and prevent assumption aversion from paralyzing change work.
