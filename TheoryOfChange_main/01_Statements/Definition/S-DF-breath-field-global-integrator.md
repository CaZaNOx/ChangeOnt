# Breath field — spiral recursion (global integrator)
## Claim (formal)
The breath field is a global integration layer organizing validation cycles across the system, coordinating RTV, SRL, and loopiness metrics to balance exploration and stabilization.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
There is a system‑level “breathing” that coordinates all the local checking, keeping cycles near enough to stay coherent while still progressing.

## Philosophical Justification
- [[S-DF-rtv-operator]] sets the cadence of validation; [[S-DF-structural-recurrence-likelihood]] measures recurrence; [[S-DF-loopiness]] tracks cycle density.
- Pure conservation collapses to circles (no novelty); pure expansion drifts linearly (lost context). Spiral recursion maintains adjacency while advancing, enabling coherent global integration.
- A global breath field aggregates local checks into a rhythm that stabilizes identity across scales.

## Explanation (informal)
Think of breath as the metronome for all validation loops. It paces how often we revisit, how far we range, and how tight the spiral stays so we don’t spin out or stagnate.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Spiral adjacency (nearby arms) supplies LocalReach continuity; extreme tightening → trivial circle; extreme loosening → drift.
- Effective field view: interpret breath as a multi‑scale integral over recursive shells, complementing the alignment field lens ([[S-DF-gauge-alignment-field]]).

## Derivation (Formal/Logical/Mathematical)
```text
Breath integrates per-cycle RTV outcomes and SRL into scheduler weights:
  Breath_t := Integrate(RTV_t, SRL_t, Loopiness_t)
driving depth/breadth toggles under constraints (ε, SE).
```
