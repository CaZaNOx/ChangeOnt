# Gap-generation law links Gödel gaps to attention and emotion operators

AI_13 spells out a practical field theory: Gödel gaps are regions where the attention field \(A(x)\) or ownership gate \(\pi(x)\) collapses, leaving parts of the manifold undefined. A simple gap-generation law classifies gaps via the gate—benign when \(\pi\) stays permeable, creative when brief blocking spawns new degrees of freedom, hazardous when \(\pi\) zeroes a neighborhood (option collapse). Role/emotion archetypes (TAG ridge, LAG lobe, GTO annulus, EXP filaments, etc.) serve as templates for how attention and urgency flows steer toward or away from these gaps. Emotion operators (Awe raises entropy; Fear tightens gates; Precision sharpens trajectories) act as controllable levers: they change the weightings on paths, predict exploration vs. exploitation behavior, and generate testable payoffs (Prediction E). Mapping these operators over the manifold produces the same gap maps described earlier—highlighting where exploration, gating, or novelty operators must intervene to keep the subject out of catastrophic Gödel zones.
