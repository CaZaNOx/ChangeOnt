# Sketch an a priori entailment from change ontology to phenomenology

## Statement (clarification)
An a priori entailment from CO to phenomenology requires explicit formal machinery: sorts for states \(S\), trajectories \(T\), subjects \(X\); a Now relation \(\operatorname{Now}(x,s)\); operators for experience-state tuples, self-indexing \(\iota_x\), learning update \(\mathcal{L}_x\); and a stable identity manifold \(M_x\) (invariants under \(F\) up to gauge). Without these, the entailment demand is ill-posed.

## Philosophical Justification
- Entailment needs fixed meanings; CO alone does not pin phenomenology unless subjects, identity conditions, and Now/experience operators are formalized.
- Identity-through-change (invariants/gauges) is necessary to define \(M_x\) and to avoid trivializing self-reference.
- Self-indexing and update operators capture the perspectival structure required for phenomenal claims.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- This is a requirements list, not a completed proof; it guards against hand-waving about “CO explains consciousness” without the formal bridge.
- Gauge choices matter: entailment must declare what counts as the same subject/experience under transformations.
- Links to hard-problem efforts: provides the template to attempt formal derivation.

## Next Steps in Chain
- suggest: instantiate the operators on a toy system and test whether phenomenal predicates become derivable.
- suggest: tie \(M_x\) and Now to empirical estimators (attention/SE, broadcast).
