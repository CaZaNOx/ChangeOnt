# Ich-attractor — minimizing Lyapunov-style field energy

## Claim (formal)
Compress the self-state via \(z_t = g(\theta_t, W_t)\) and define an energy \(\Phi(z;U,V,H)\) over urgency, volatility, and context. The Ich-attractor \(\mathcal{A} = \arg\min_z \Phi(z;U,V,H)\) captures the metastable “self” when \(U_t\) is moderate, \(A_t,B_t\) high, and the time kernel spans enabling windows. Sudden rises in \(U_t\) or \(\lambda_t\) lower energy barriers and cause attractor jumps (dreams, crisis, therapy).

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
The felt “me” is a metastable attractor shaped by current broadcast, reflection, and arousal/volatility; shocks can flip it.

## Philosophical Justification
- [[S-DF-global-broadcast]] provides the shared present state; [[S-DF-reflection-operator]] closes past-present-future; SE sets basin depth.
- Modeling the self as an attractor makes presence testable via energy proxies rather than metaphor.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Energy terms (U,V,H) should be instantiated per domain; tracking flips helps audit stability.
- Link to SE to prevent over-reading transient minima as “self.”
