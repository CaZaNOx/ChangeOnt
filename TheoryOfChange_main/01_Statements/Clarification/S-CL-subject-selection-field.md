# Subject cuts depend on local fields and Fisher-weighted learning

Lines 19356‑19419 show where “Gödel holes” live relative to subjects: no projection \(\pi_S\) makes the induced dynamics Markov, producing irreducible closure gaps. Meta-flow shrinks them via explicit modeling. The subject learns \(\pi_S\) (plus attention weights \(a_S\)) to maximize predictive sufficiency under the local field—formally an information bottleneck objective. The Fisher-information metric \(g_{ij}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}[\partial_i \log p(y|\theta)\,\partial_j \log p(y|\theta)]\) governs how attention gravitates toward sensitive regions; selection today updates the parameters \(\phi_t\), changing tomorrow’s cut. Thus selection is field-dependent by construction, and that dependence keeps the subject adaptive.
