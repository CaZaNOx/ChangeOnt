# Gödel navigation calculus profiles personas and meta-operators

AI_13 extends the gap policy into a navigation calculus: steering into Gödel holes requires a balance of operators (divide/map/simulate/approximate), comfort with underspecified problems, and personas (stabilizers vs. explorers) that can be measured and trained. The calculus logs “fork → bend → merge → renormalize” cycles, tracks curvature at the Gödel front, and scores people by how often they reweight their operator basis when facing underspecified regions. This makes “steering into Gödel holes” a composable competency rather than a vibe—teams can track how much time they spend in stabilizing vs. probing modes, and design training to raise their change-intellect (ability to invent new variables and bridge attractors when gap pressure rises).
