# Final gap list—conserved quantities, operational holes, and process-first calculus

The closing checklist (lines 20040‑20130) calls for: (1) naming conserved quantities (selfness, prediction-error budgets) and proving symmetry ⇒ invariant ⇒ attractor; (2) replacing the physics→chemistry→biology→mind sketch with explicit coarse-graining functors; (3) defining Gödel hole density via measure-theoretic continuation gaps or conditional mutual information; (4) building toy labs to show residual collapse only after representation expansion; (5) fixing the change-operator as a set-valued process on metric spaces with viability kernels, hole sets \( \mathcal{C}_t(x)=\varnothing \), and reflexive updates; (6) using process-first formalisms (viability theory, differential inclusions, evolving spaces) to avoid sneaking in classical fixed-state assumptions. This is the remaining work before CO counts as a full scientific calculus.
