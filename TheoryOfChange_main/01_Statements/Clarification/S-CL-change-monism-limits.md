 # Change monism removes substance dualism but still requires invariants and proofs

## Statement (clarification)
CO is monist: being = changing; no physics‑vs‑qualia dual stuff. Selves are metastable invariants of self‑modulating flows, with indexicals included in the physical description. This rejects “swap the code freely” thought experiments that ignore embodied coupling. However, monism alone does not explain why this flow feels this way; CO still owes explicit invariants, uniqueness/rigidity arguments, and predictions about conscious patterns.

## Philosophical Justification
- From ongoing change plus identity‑through‑change, “self” is an invariant pattern, not a separate substance.
- Indexicals are part of the same flow; removing them yields an incomplete physical description.
- Monism is necessary to dissolve pseudo dualism, but explanatory adequacy demands pattern‑picking criteria and predictions, not mere rhetoric.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Needed: indexical anchoring, rigidity proofs, audit of invariants that pick out conscious flows.
- Analogy: fluid equations single out vortices; CO must similarly single out conscious patterns.
- Blocks code‑swapping intuitions that ignore environment/body coupling and SE budgets.

## Next Steps in Chain
- suggest: develop explicit invariants for conscious patterns and tie them to SE/RTV tests.
- suggest: link to hard‑problem clarifications and gauge experiment plans.
