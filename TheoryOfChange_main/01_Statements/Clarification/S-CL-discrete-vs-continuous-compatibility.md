# Discrete-change worlds remain compatible via kernels and aggregation

AI_13 explains that continuity isn’t logically forced; it depends on adopting refinability. A discrete-time Change Ontology (DCT) can satisfy “change is happening now” by requiring non-stasis across ticks, variable distinction sets \(D_t\), and observation windows aggregating \(K\) ticks, which yields smooth phenomenology (like digital audio). Tension corresponds to out-degree in the transition graph; meta-change is kernel drift. Gödel-pressure becomes growth of prediction error under current coarse models. To call a “center” continuous you must adopt refinability or coarse-graining assumptions; otherwise discrete jumps remain coherent. The bottom line: CO works with discrete processes by treating continuity as an optional module (“effective continuity”) rather than a metaphysical necessity.
