# Identity — metastable self-similarity across transformations
## Claim (formal)
An entity is a metastable, recursively self‑similar pattern of relations persisting across transformations within continuous change.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
“Being the same” is not about freezing; it is about preserving enough recognizable structure across ongoing difference. Identity is a pattern breathing through change.

## Philosophical Justification
In a world where Δ never ceases, persistence cannot be literal stasis. We therefore locate identity in what persists as recognizable under transformation: relational configurations that recur. This explains how persistence and novelty coexist: identity is achieved as recursive stabilization, not assumed as a primitive. Reach and LocalReach (neighbors under Δ) supply the admissible transforms; similarity thresholds make “≈” explicit.

## Explanation (informal)
Self‑similarity summarizes that successive transformations map a pattern into close variants of itself (≈) under stated criteria; composition of such maps yields persistence over larger spans.

## Derivation (Philosophical)
- From [[S-FT-continuity-noncessation]]: change does not stop, so identity must live inside Δ.
- From [[S-DF-reach-relation]] and [[S-DF-localreach-topology]]: persistence is defined over admissible transformations within LocalReach.
- Introduce ≈ (declared invariants, tolerances) to mark “same enough”; require recursive closure of ≈ over the persistence window to qualify as identity.

## Derivation (Formal/Logical/Mathematical)
```text
Let T_k be transformations in LocalReach.
Identity(P) := ∀k∈K: T_k(P) ≈ P, with K spanning a persistence window.
```

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Criteria for ≈ must be stated (invariants, tolerances ε, σ(ε)).
- Identity ≡ is context‑dependent but disciplined by declared invariants.
- Proto‑identity markers: use a small set of coherence markers (invariants, ≈ thresholds, SE trends) to track short‑lived identity without conflating it with personal identity claims.
- Identity collapses if SE falls below [[S-DF-stabilization-energy]] thresholds or if ≈ fails across the window.
