# Gödel gaps mark novelty frontiers and boost probe policies

## Statement (clarification)
Gödel gaps mark novelty frontiers: treating unknowns as structured gaps and using hedge/probe/jump policies yields >2× uplift versus ignoring them. Higher probe-policy scores produce more distinct strategies in creative gaps. Identities act as attractors; attention weights paths; incompleteness signals when to switch policies.

## Philosophical Justification
- From GH framing, gaps are where closure fails; treating them as structured targets turns incompleteness into a driver for exploration.
- Attractors/attention provide the mechanics: gaps pull resources toward frontier regions where new skills/layers are minted.
- Empirical uplift (policy-driven gains) aligns with change-fitness: adaptivity at holes improves performance.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Creative vs hazardous gaps must be distinguished; policies differ (probe vs hedge).
- Metrics: probe-policy scores, strategy diversity in gap-rich tasks; should be logged in audits.
- Connects phenomenology (feeling of openness) to operational behavior (policy switch).

## Next Steps in Chain
- suggest: formalize probe/hedge/jump policies with GH metrics and test them.
- suggest: integrate gap scoring with attention routers to automate policy shifts.
