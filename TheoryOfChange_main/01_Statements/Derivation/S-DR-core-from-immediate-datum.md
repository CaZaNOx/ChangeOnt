# From Immediate Datum to tolerance, gauge, attractors, and classical limit

## Claim (formal)
Starting from the Immediate Datum and finitude, the core CO constructs follow: (1) arisal implies discernible Δ-now; (2) bounded discrimination forces a bend metric and tolerance τ to keep “same-enough” identity coherent; (3) scarcity of attention/energy requires a gauge that warps which bends are negligible; (4) gauge plus tolerance yields attractors/phase flips (black-hole basins); (5) setting τ→0 and a flat gauge collapses the ontology to the classical slice (shortest-path/Boolean).

## Philosophical Translation
Because we can only sense imperfectly, we need a graded sameness (tolerance) and a way to reweight what matters (gauge). Those together explain habits and ruts; if we turn off tolerance and gauge, we recover classical logic and shortest-path behavior.

## Derivation (sketch)
- Immediate Datum (FT) → arisal: there is a felt Δ between now and just-prior; re-identification is required to avoid total collapse of “same thing” (phenomenology: shoe-tying variations).
- Finitude → tolerance τ: identity must permit small bends; otherwise every micro-variation breaks sameness. Bend metric gives minimal correction; quotient classes define identities up to budget τ.
- Scarcity → gauge: selective attention must warp perceived bend costs to avoid wasting budget on irrelevant differences; Robbins–Monro is minimal online update.
- Gauge + tolerance → attractors/phase flips: concentrated gauge deepens basins (Δt flips sign), making small deviations cheaper to bend back than to escape—explains “skills click” and ruts.
- Classical limit: τ→0, flat gauge ⇒ bend metric reduces to equality; quantale collapses to Boolean (via [[01_Statements/Derivation/S-DR-quantale-boolean-flattening-proof]]); path choice reduces to classical shortest-path/MDP.
