# Guard against gauge inflation and measurement drift in phenomenology

The blunt audit at lines 17323‑17350 warns that the gauge/invariant/transport program can fail in two ways. Gauge too big ⇒ success becomes empty; fix by preregistering \(G\) tightly (e.g., only isometries for local geometry, Lipschitz-bounded monotone warps for affect) and reporting the residual gauge error. Invariants too narrow ⇒ Goodharting: non-conscious systems might satisfy the metrics without genuine presence; combat by combining transport tests, lesion logic (predicting specific degradations when integration/affect/self-model are knocked out), and multimodal data so ratings, behavior, and physiology cohere. Subjective ratings can drift or be strategic, so align them with objective probes and treat language leakage as signal to refine the canonical space.
