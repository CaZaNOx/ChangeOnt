# Honest audit of the identity bridge highlights remaining commitments

AI_13’s “honest audit” section (lines 16678‑16710) spells out the costs of the bridge law: (1) it is an a posteriori identity postulate; there is no derivation from non-mental vocabulary, so the commitment must be owned; (2) grain selection has to be fixed empirically—what temporal window \(\tau\) and spatial extent of \(G^*\) define a subject, and what scale invariants persist across REM, anesthesia, psychedelics, newborns; (3) multiple realizability must be handled by category-equivalence proofs or other demonstrations that non-isomorphic mechanisms preserving the invariants yield the same phenomenology; and (4) if the bridge cracks, fallback closures include phenomenal-concepts strategies (conceptual isolation) or minimal pan-experiential change monism. Without confronting these items the “closure” claim is ungrounded.
