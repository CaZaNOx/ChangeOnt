# Program evaluation highlights strengths and missing pieces

AI_13’s candid status report (lines 18995‑19036) calls out what works and what doesn’t: the geometry/dynamics/topology framing plus urgency-as-policy-shaping is promising, but Gödel holes remain a metaphor until operationalized; bridge laws from latent manifolds to felt qualities are vague; identifiability is a risk; the “hard problem” is dissolved for naturalists but won’t satisfy dualists. Remaining tasks include defining a computational Gödel-hole operator, publishing registered predictions (geodesic length ↔ RT, curvature peaks ↔ interference, etc.), showing representation expansion after persistent residuals, and tightening originality/feasibility claims with real experiments.
