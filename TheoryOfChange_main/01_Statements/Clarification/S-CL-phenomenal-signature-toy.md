# Toy signature shows how opponent pairs and curvature become definable

AI_13 sketches a toy scheme: decompose \(V_x\) into typed errors \(e_1,\dots,e_m\) and a controllability tensor \(\mathcal{C}\), define a phenomenal signature \(\mathrm{Sig}_x(s)\) from the dominant error, the sign of opponent pairs \((e_p,e_q)\), and curvature \(\kappa_C(\mathcal{A}_x(s))\) (all normalized by the normal form). Prove that opponent pairs exist/are unique up to sign, that \(\kappa_C\) is gauge-invariant, and that any CO-automorphism preserves \(\mathrm{Sig}\). Then “red” becomes a definable case (first opponent pair positive on the chromatic manifold). This illustrates how role-defined predicates can become theorems once the invariants are fixed.
