# Hypothesis — Function/ant/Collatz algebra via CO attractors

Claim (speculative)
- There may be a CO-native algebra over dynamical primitives (Langton’s ant, Collatz-like maps) that predicts attractor basins and interaction outcomes by composing their “motifs” (loopiness, drift, branching) rather than step-by-step simulation.

Exploratory sketch
- Define motif features for a discrete dynamical system (e.g., bend/branch budget, loop density, symmetry). Compose motifs (sum/product/star analogues) to predict resultant attractor type/shape on a given substrate (grid/topology/coloring). Check whether compositions predict basins for combined rules vs. brute-force simulation.

Falsifiers
- Motif composition yields no predictive power over baseline simulation.
- Degenerates to existing cellular automata algebra with no added structure.

Why it matters
- If viable, could give qualitative/quantitative handles on chaotic-looking systems (ants/Collatz) and a way to reason about “sum”/“interaction” of dynamical rules in CO terms.
