# Frame-level change (Tx) violates Markov closure
## Claim (formal)
If transitions can map (S, Frame)→(S', Frame'), then modeling closure on a fixed S fails. Therefore Tx implies nonclosure relative to fixed-space Markov assumptions.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
When the “space of possibilities” itself can change, you cannot pretend it stays fixed without losing the essence of the change.

## Clarifications / Further Context
- Nonclosure is not a failure but a feature: it marks where meta‑operators are required.
