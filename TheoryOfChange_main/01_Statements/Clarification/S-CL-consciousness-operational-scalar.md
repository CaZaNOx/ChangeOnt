# Conscious access can be operationalized by a thick-present coupling scalar

AI_13 first states what change ontology requires of consciousness (lines 16786‑16792): it must emerge lawfully from change, be representable on the state manifold, form metastable attractors, and causally modulate other change. The “minimal, testable consciousness sketch” then defines concrete knobs—urgency \(U_t\), broadcast clarity \(B_t\), reflexive access \(A_t\), thick-present dwell \(\tau\)—and proposes an operational scalar
\[
\mathcal{C}_t = I(b_t;\text{Modules}) \times I(b_t;m_t) \times \text{dwell}(b_t) \times \mathrm{Var}(b_t)
\]
that detects when the broadcast globally couples modules, includes the self in the loop, sustains availability, and lets valence steer updates. Conscious episodes correspond to regimes where \(\mathcal{C}_t\) exceeds thresholds. The same sketch lists falsifiable predictions: perturb \(U_t\) to co-vary \(\mathcal{C}_t\) and behavioral flexibility, lesion re-entrant broadcast \(G\) to decouple perception from report, boost \(I(b_t;m_t)\) to increase metacognitive calibration without changing d′, and flip \(\operatorname{sgn}(e_t)\) to control valence/learning rates. When those manipulations fail, the consciousness claim collapses.
