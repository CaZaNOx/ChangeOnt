# Subject arisal ground — reflexive continuity of identity
## Claim (formal)
A subject arises when a metastable identity loop crosses self‑similarity thresholds and maintains sufficient stabilization energy (SE) to support reflexive evaluation (about‑itself). Let I(t) denote identity coherence; if I(t) ≥ θ_id and SE ≥ θ_SE over windows longer than the local breath period, a reflexive locus S emerges enabling awareness/experience relations.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
The “someone” shows up when a pattern holds itself together long enough, with enough integrity, to take itself as something — and then to relate to what it encounters.

## Philosophical Justification
- [[S-DF-identity-through-change]]: identity is a story of continuity under Δ; subject needs that base.
- [[S-DF-self-similarity-threshold]]: without sufficient self‑match, no stable locus to host awareness.
- [[S-DF-stabilization-energy]]: maintaining coherence costs energy/tension; below threshold reflexivity collapses.
- Reflexivity (aboutness of the loop to itself) is the minimal criterion distinguishing subject from generic dynamic pattern.

## Explanation (informal)
Take any changing system. If its dynamics sustain a recognizable pattern, and that pattern can re‑enter its own state estimates while staying coherent, a subject-locus appears. This is the ground of later self‑models and agency; it precedes rich narrative selves.

## Derivation (Philosophical)
- From continuity under change → persistence of invariants.
- From persistence + similarity threshold → recognizable self.
- From recognizable self + sufficient SE → space for reflexive tracking.

## Derivation (Formal/Logical/Mathematical)
```text
Given I(t) ≥ θ_id over window W and SE(W) ≥ θ_SE,
∃S : Reflexive(S) ∧ Bears(S, Awareness)
```

## Proofs/Corollaries References
- proof: constructive: show minimal loop satisfying identity + SE supports reflexive mapping.
- corollary: supports [[S-DF-subject-recursive-field]], [[S-DF-self-model-evolution]].

## Clarifications / Further Context
- This is a ground condition: it does not presume a rich self‑model; it enables it.
- Links forward to subject-as-field and self‑model evolution, backward to identity‑through‑change.
- Breath period reference: window must exceed local integration constant to avoid transient “pseudo‑subjects.”
- Does not require semantic content; only reflexive structural mapping.
