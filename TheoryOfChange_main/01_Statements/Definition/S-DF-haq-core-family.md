# Harmonic Attractor Quantization (HAQ) — core family
## Claim (formal)
HAQ denotes families of discrete/quantized attractors arising from recursive self‑similarity constraints and stabilization pressures (SE, bend), yielding preferred modes of persistence.

## Philosophical Translation (of formal claim)
Some patterns “ring true” in discrete ways — they recur in harmonics of stability.

## Philosophical Justification
- [[S-DF-identity-through-change]]: identity needs recurring self-similarity; quantization captures discrete stable modes.
- [[S-DF-stabilization-energy]]: SE shapes which modes persist; low SE prunes unstable modes.
- [[S-DF-prm-bend-metric]]: bend cost defines curvature of trajectories; harmonics arise where bend/SE balance.

## Explanation (informal)
HAQ is the family name for attractor “notes” a system can sustain. Each note is a stable pattern; the family is the palette of such notes under given SE/bend constraints.

## Derivation (Philosophical)
- Consider identity dynamics with bend costs and SE budgets.
- Quantized attractors emerge where returning trajectories resonate; classify these as HAQ modes.

## Proofs/Corollaries References
- feeds [[S-DF-attractor-field]]; specialized in HAQ elements (EA/ED/EF...).

## Clarifications / Further Context
- HAQ is not a single attractor but a family parameterized by SE, bend, and invariants.
- Links to gauge choices and warp elements for adaptive stability.
