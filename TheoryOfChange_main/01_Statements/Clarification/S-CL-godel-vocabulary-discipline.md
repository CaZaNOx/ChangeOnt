# Reserve “Gödel” for formal limits and anchor it with a glossary

AI_13’s editorial pass (lines 22365‑22578) insists that Gödel language stay crisp: build a 6‑8 line glossary for datum, pointer, cut, givenness operator \( \mathsf{G} \), refinement, and Gödel hole; separate phenomenology, logic, and modeling layers; and trim Gödel references in notes that lack a formal role. When you need the term, tie it to concrete “model-internal blind spots” (limits of description classes) rather than as proof of metaphysical truths. Otherwise quarantine the metaphor until a “Levels & Limits” note spells out the precise role. This keeps Change Ontology readable (no loose “you” voice), prevents term drift between phenomenological, structural, and physical senses of “change,” and makes every Gödel invocation auditable.
